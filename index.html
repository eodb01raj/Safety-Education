Welcome to Fire Safety Training

Welcome to Fire Safety Training. Your safety is our priority. This training will guide you through the essential steps to take in a fire emergency.

Key Features of This Training:

  • Learn essential fire safety procedures
  • Understand emergency evacuation protocols
  • Practice proper use of safety equipment
  • Test your knowledge with interactive quiz

Recognizing the Fire Alarm

Know the sound of the fire alarm. Upon hearing it, you must act immediately.

Key Points:

  • Fire alarms have a distinct, loud sound
  • Never ignore a fire alarm
  • Act immediately when you hear it
  • Assume it's a real emergency

The R.A.C.E. Protocol

Remember to R.A.C.E. - Rescue, Alarm, Confine, Evacuate.

R.A.C.E. Steps:

  • R - Rescue anyone in immediate danger
  • A - Alarm - Activate fire alarm and call emergency
  • C - Confine the fire by closing doors
  • E - Evacuate the building safely

R - Rescue

RESCUE anyone in immediate danger from the fire, if it does not endanger your own life.

Key Points:

  • Only rescue if it's safe to do so
  • Don't put yourself in danger
  • Help those who cannot help themselves
  • Move people away from immediate danger

A - Alarm

ALARM: Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station and call emergency services.

Key Points:

  • Pull the nearest fire alarm station
  • Call emergency services (911)
  • Alert others in the building
  • Provide clear location information

C - Confine

CONFINE the fire by closing all doors and windows as you leave.

Key Points:

  • Close doors behind you as you evacuate
  • Close windows if safe to do so
  • This helps contain the fire and smoke
  • Slows the spread of fire to other areas

E - Evacuate

EVACUATE the building immediately. Do not use elevators.

Key Points:

  • Leave the building immediately
  • Never use elevators during a fire
  • Use stairs only
  • Stay low if there's smoke
  • Feel doors before opening them

Evacuation Routes

Follow the designated primary evacuation routes. If blocked, use a secondary route.

Key Points:

  • Know your primary evacuation route
  • Always have a backup route planned
  • Follow evacuation route signs
  • Stay calm and move quickly but safely

Emergency Exits

Emergency exits must always be kept clear. Never block them.

Key Points:

  • Keep all emergency exits clear at all times
  • Never store items near exit doors
  • Ensure exit signs are visible and lit
  • Report blocked exits immediately

Assembly Point

Proceed to the designated assembly point after evacuating. Wait for instructions.

Key Points:

  • Know your designated assembly point location
  • Go directly there after evacuating
  • Stay at the assembly point until cleared
  • Wait for further instructions from emergency personnel

Using a Fire Extinguisher (P.A.S.S.)

For small fires only. Remember P.A.S.S: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep.

P.A.S.S. Method:

  • P - Pull the pin from the handle
  • A - Aim at the base of the fire
  • S - Squeeze the handle
  • S - Sweep from side to side

Fire Exit Definitions

In fire exit rules, "horizontal exit" means an arrangement which allows alternative egress from a floor area to another floor at or near the same level in an adjoining building or an adjoining part of the same building with adequate separation; and "travel distance" means the distance an occupant has to travel to reach an exit.

Key Definitions:

  • Horizontal exit: Alternative egress to adjoining building at same level
  • Travel distance: Distance occupant travels to reach an exit

Types of Fire Exits

An exit may be a doorway, corridor, passageway to an internal or external stairway or to a verandah. An exit may also include a horizontal exit leading to an adjoining building at the same level.

Exit Types:

  • Doorway exits
  • Corridor and passageway exits
  • Internal and external stairway exits
  • Verandah exits

Not Considered as Exits

Lifts, escalators and revolving doors shall not be considered as exits for the purpose of fire safety regulations.

NOT Fire Exits:

  • Lifts/Elevators - can malfunction during fire
  • Escalators - can stop working during emergency
  • Revolving doors - can jam and trap people

Travel Distance Requirements

Exits shall be so located that the travel distance on the floor shall not exceed 30 meters.

Key Requirements:

  • Maximum 30 meters travel distance to exit
  • Measured from any point on the floor
  • Applies to normal factory conditions

Exit Width Requirements

The unit of exit width used to measure capacity of any exit shall be 50 cm. A clear width of 25 cm shall be counted as an additional half unit. Clear width of less than 25 cm shall not be counted for exit width.

Width Standards:

  • 1 unit = 50 cm width
  • 0.5 unit = 25 cm additional width
  • Less than 25 cm = not counted

Sufficient Exits

In every room of a factory sufficient exits to permit safe escape of the occupants in case of fire or other emergency shall be provided which shall be free of any obstruction.

Key Requirements:

  • Every room must have sufficient exits for safe escape
  • Exits must be free of any obstruction
  • Applies to fire and other emergencies

Exit Visibility & Illumination

The exits shall be clearly visible and suitably illuminated with suitable arrangement, whatever artificial lighting is to be adopted for this purpose, to maintain the required illumination in case of failure of the normal source of electric supply.

Key Requirements:

  • Exits must be clearly visible at all times
  • Suitable illumination required
  • Backup lighting for power failure

Exit Marking Requirements

The exits shall be marked in a language understood by the majority of the workers.

Key Requirements:

  • Exits must be clearly marked
  • Use language understood by majority of workers
  • Consider local language requirements

Fire Resisting Doors

Fire resisting doors or roller shutters shall be provided at appropriate places along the escape routes to prevent spread of fire and smoke, particularly at the entrance of lifts or stairs where funnel or flue effect may be created inducing an upward spread of fire.

Key Requirements:

  • Fire resisting doors at appropriate places
  • Prevent spread of fire and smoke
  • Critical at lift and stair entrances
  • Prevent funnel/flue effect

Continuous Means of Egress

All exits shall provide continuous means of egress to the exterior of a building or to an exterior open space leading to a street.

Key Requirements:

  • Continuous path to building exterior
  • No dead ends or interruptions
  • Must lead to street or open space
